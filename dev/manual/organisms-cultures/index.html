Organisms & Cultures

A Culture also tracks living organisms, not just chemicals – an Organism is a species-and-strain identity: genus, species, and strain.

Registering an organism

Register one with @organism:

@organism SMU_UA159 "Streptococcus" "mutans" "UA159"
julia> genus(SMU_UA159)
"Streptococcus"

julia> species(SMU_UA159)
"mutans"

julia> strain(SMU_UA159)
"UA159"

julia> name(SMU_UA159)
"Streptococcus mutans UA159"

name(x) joins all three fields for display. show(x) prints the recoverable binding name instead (SMU_UA159), the same convention Reagent/Chemical use.

Recalling with @org_str

@org_str is the collision-safe lookup, mirroring @loc_str/@attr_str/@rgt_str/@chem_str:

julia> org"SMU_UA159"
SMU_UA159

Promoting a Stock to a Culture

Adding an Organism to any Stock – e.g. saline, built in Stocks – promotes it to a Culture:

julia> culture = saline + org"SMU_UA159"
