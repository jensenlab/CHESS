Recipes & Solution Chemistry

Note

This chapter's chemistry model is intentionally simple – pitched at what a biology lab needs (dissociation into ions, molar concentration, pH), not a complete physical chemistry treatment. Activity coefficients, ionic strength, and buffer/equilibrium effects are out of scope; pH here is a direct estimate from net H⁺/OH⁻ concentration, not a full equilibrium calculation.

A Stock is measured in Reagents – physical things you weigh out. Recipe reduces that to real molar quantities of Chemicals instead, accounting for dissociation, derived one-directionally via recipe(s::Stock):

julia> r = recipe(saline)
Recipe(Dict(Cl⁻ => 0.0856 mol, Na⁺ => 0.0856 mol, water => 0.0555 mol))

water itself is in there too – recipe sums every reagent's contribution, dissociating or not. water doesn't dissociate, so its only contribution is its own identity, per composition's default from the previous chapters.

Reading a Recipe

mass and molar_amount read a Recipe's quantity of a given Chemical:

julia> mass(r, Na⁺)
1.97 g

julia> molar_amount(r, Na⁺)
0.0856 mol

total_concentration

The molar concentration of a Chemical across the whole stock:

julia> total_concentration(saline, Na⁺)
0.0856 mol mL⁻¹

pH and net_hydrogen_ion_concentration

pH is derived from net_hydrogen_ion_concentration, which nets the canonical H⁺/OH⁻ Chemicals – introduced in Reagents & Chemicals – against each other. saline is a neutral salt, so it comes out flat:

julia> pH(saline)
7.0

A new reagent, registered here specifically to show this meaningfully:

@reagent_formula HCl "hydrochloric acid" Liquid (H⁺+Cl⁻) 1.18u"g/mL" missing
julia> acid = 1u"mL"*HCl + 100u"mL"*water

julia> net_hydrogen_ion_concentration(acid)
0.00032 mol mL⁻¹

julia> pH(acid)
0.49

This explicit H⁺-minus-OH⁻ subtraction is exactly why a base registers its real dissociation formula (e.g. Na⁺ + OH⁻ for NaOH) rather than a negative H⁺ count – CompositionRule coefficients must stay non-negative, and mixing an acid and a base nets out through this subtraction instead.
