Stocks

A Stock is a combination of organisms and chemicals – what actually lives inside a Well. Stock is an abstract type; which concrete subtype you get is determined by what it contains, not chosen directly: Empty (nothing), Mixture (solids only), Solution (at least one liquid, any solids), Culture (at least one organism, any solids/liquids – covered in Organisms & Cultures). The generic Stock(organisms,solids,liquids) constructor automatically picks the right one, checking in order whether any organisms, then liquids, then solids are present:

OrganismsLiquidsSolidsResult
≥ 1anyanyCulture
0≥ 1anySolution
00≥ 1Mixture
000Empty
julia> Empty()
Empty Stock

Building a stock from a quantity

The natural way to build one is multiplying a quantity by a Reagent:

julia> water_solution = 1u"mL" * water
1.0 mL Solution (1 reagent(s))
 Liquids  Name   Amount  Concentration
───────────────────────────────────────
 water    water  1.0 mL        100.0 %

julia> salt = 5u"g" * NaCl
5.0 g Mixture (1 reagent(s))
 Solids  Name             Amount  Concentration
────────────────────────────────────────────────
 NaCl    sodium chloride   5.0 g        100.0 %

Mixing with +

Combining two stocks via + always produces whichever subtype the combined contents call for – water plus salt is genuinely saline now, so that's what this one gets called:

julia> saline = water_solution + salt
1.0 mL Solution (2 reagent(s))
 Solids  Name             Amount  Concentration
────────────────────────────────────────────────
 NaCl    sodium chloride   5.0 g     5.0 g mL⁻¹

 Liquids  Name   Amount  Concentration
───────────────────────────────────────
 water    water  1.0 mL        100.0 %

quantity(::Stock) reports total liquid volume only (1.0 mL here, not counting the dissolved solid's mass). Solids contribute to volume_estimate instead, which falls back to density-based estimation and warns when a solid's density is unknown – as it is for NaCl here, registered with a missing density in Reagents & Chemicals:

julia> volume_estimate(salt)
┌ Warning: volume_estimate: density unknown for sodium chloride; excluded from the estimate (result is a lower bound)
└ @ CHESSCore ...
0.0 mL

Scaling

*// by a plain number scales every reagent proportionally; multiplying by a quantity instead scales the whole stock to hit that quantity as its new total:

julia> double = 2*saline
2.0 mL Solution (2 reagent(s))
 Solids  Name             Amount  Concentration
────────────────────────────────────────────────
 NaCl    sodium chloride  10.0 g     5.0 g mL⁻¹

 Liquids  Name   Amount  Concentration
───────────────────────────────────────
 water    water  2.0 mL        100.0 %

julia> tenmL = 10u"mL" * saline
10.0 mL Solution (2 reagent(s))
 Solids  Name             Amount  Concentration
────────────────────────────────────────────────
 NaCl    sodium chloride  50.0 g     5.0 g mL⁻¹

 Liquids  Name   Amount   Concentration
────────────────────────────────────────
 water    water  10.0 mL        100.0 %

The non-negativity constraint

- mixes by subtraction, and throws MixingError if any reagent would go negative:

julia> saline - double
ERROR: MixingError: NaCl: attempted to add a negative quantity to a Stock

@stock_str for named recipes

Register one with @stock (mirroring @location_kind/@reagent/ @chemical/@organism), then recall it with @stock_str. Unlike a bare const binding, only recipes registered this way are reachable – an ordinary intermediate Stock (e.g. a concentrated stock solution combined into a larger recipe) never accidentally becomes discoverable:

@stock saline_recipe 1u"mL" * water + 5u"g" * NaCl
julia> stock"saline_recipe"
